Our Shower Installation Process

Creating watertight, beautiful showers step by step

1

Design & Planning

We measure your space, discuss design options, and help select tile. We'll plan niches, benches, and special features. 3D visualization available for complex designs.

2

Demo & Waterproofing

Careful demolition protects your bathroom. We install cement board, apply waterproof membrane to all surfaces, and test for complete water protection.

3

Tile Installation

Starting from the bottom up, we install each tile perfectly level and aligned. Custom cuts around fixtures, perfect grout lines, and attention to every detail.

4

Grouting & Sealing

Color-matched grout application, thorough cleaning, and premium sealers protect your investment. We install fixtures and ensure everything works perfectly.

Shower Installation FAQs

Common questions about shower projects

We use premium waterproof membrane systems like Schluter or RedGard, applied to all shower surfaces including the pan. This creates a completely waterproof envelope behind your tile. We also use waterproof backer board and proper slope to ensure water flows to the drain. Every shower is water-tested before tiling.

Most shower installations take 3-5 days: Day 1 for demo and prep, Day 2 for waterproofing and pan, Days 3-4 for tile installation, and Day 5 for grouting and finishing. Larger or more complex showers may take longer. You can typically use your shower 24 hours after final grout sealing.

Yes! Tub-to-shower conversions are one of our specialties. We remove the tub, adjust plumbing as needed, and create a beautiful walk-in shower. We can add features like benches, grab bars, and curbless entry for accessibility. Most conversions take 4-6 days.

It depends on your style and shower size. Large format tiles (12x24" or larger) create a modern, spacious look with fewer grout lines. Classic subway tile (3x6") offers timeless appeal. For shower floors, we recommend smaller tiles (2x2" or mosaic) for better slip resistance and proper drainage slope.
