Why Choose Laminate Flooring?

AC4 and AC5 rated laminate handles heavy traffic, pet claws, and active kids. The tough wear layer protects against daily wear and tear.

Realistic Textures

Our Laminate Installation Process

Professional installation in 4 simple steps

1

Free Consultation & Measurement

Laminate Flooring FAQs

Everything you need to know about laminate installation

Traditional laminate is water-resistant but not waterproof. However, newer waterproof laminate options are available that can handle spills and moisture. For truly waterproof needs (like basements), we often recommend luxury vinyl plank instead, but waterproof laminate is a great middle-ground option.

Quality laminate flooring typically lasts 15-25 years with proper care. The lifespan depends on the AC rating (wear layer thickness), foot traffic, and maintenance. Commercial-grade AC5 laminate can last 25+ years even in high-traffic areas. Most manufacturers offer 15-30 year warranties.

Yes! Most modern laminate flooring is compatible with radiant heating systems. The key is choosing laminate rated for radiant heat and maintaining proper temperature limits (usually under 85°F). We'll ensure proper installation with appropriate underlayment for optimal heat transfer.

Laminate has a wood fiberboard core with a photographic layer and wear layer on top. It's harder and sounds more like real wood but isn't waterproof. Vinyl is 100% synthetic and waterproof throughout. Laminate is often more affordable and has a more authentic wood feel underfoot.

Yes, underlayment is essential for laminate flooring. It provides moisture protection, sound reduction, and a smooth surface for installation. Some premium laminate comes with attached underlayment, but we'll assess your specific needs. For concrete subfloors, a vapor barrier is also required.
