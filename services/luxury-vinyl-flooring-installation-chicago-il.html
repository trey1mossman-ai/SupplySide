Vinyl Flooring FAQs

Common questions about luxury vinyl installation

LVP (Luxury Vinyl Plank) mimics hardwood with long plank shapes and wood grain patterns. LVT (Luxury Vinyl Tile) replicates stone or ceramic tile in square or rectangular shapes. Both are 100% waterproof and use the same click-lock installation system. The choice is purely aesthetic based on your design preferences.

In many cases, yes! Vinyl can be installed over concrete, plywood, existing vinyl, and even some tile floors if they're level and in good condition. However, we always inspect your subfloor first. Carpet must be removed, and very uneven surfaces need leveling for warranty protection and proper installation.

Quality luxury vinyl flooring typically lasts 15-25 years with proper care. Commercial-grade options can last even longer. Unlike hardwood, vinyl won't need refinishing and maintains its appearance throughout its lifespan. Most manufacturers offer 20-30 year residential warranties on their products.

Absolutely! Vinyl is ideal for Chicago basements because it's 100% waterproof and won't be damaged by moisture or humidity. Unlike carpet or hardwood, vinyl can handle minor flooding without permanent damage. We always perform moisture tests and may recommend a vapor barrier for below-grade installations.

Vinyl is the easiest flooring to maintain! Regular sweeping or vacuuming and occasional damp mopping with a pH-neutral cleaner is all you need. No waxing, refinishing, or special treatments required. Avoid harsh chemicals and abrasive cleaners. Place mats at entrances to minimize dirt and use furniture pads to prevent scratches.
